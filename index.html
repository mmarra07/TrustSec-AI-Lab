TrustSec AI Lab

Advancing the intersection of AI and Cybersecurity

Our Mission

We develop robust and resilient artificial intelligence (AI) models to detect, withstand, and mitigate emerging AI-driven cyber threats. Our work ensures digital systems remain reliable and worthy of trust across societal and mission-critical operations.

Research Focus Areas

Adversarial AI

Designing defenses to protect AI systems—such as fraud detection and network intrusion sensors—from adversarial manipulation and attacks.

Large Language Models

Identifying vulnerabilities to prompt-injection and jailbreaking while mitigating hallucinations in RAG pipelines.

IoT & Privacy

Enhancing privacy in health-centric IoT ecosystems using generative models for anonymization and differential privacy.

Network Security

Threat detection across complex, distributed environments using deep learning and behavioral analytics.

Professional Profile

Dr. Alhussien is an Assistant Professor in the Department of Cybersecurity at the College of Emergency Preparedness, Homeland Security, and Cybersecurity, University at Albany (SUNY). She directs the TrustSec AI Lab and is an active member of the Women in Cybersecurity (WiCyS) community.

Her research has been published in leading venues such as IEEE Transactions and presented at conferences including IEEE NOMS and TrustCom.
